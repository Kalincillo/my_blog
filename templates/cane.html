White cane

Background

Since ancient times, blind people have sought different ways to overcome their limitations, but it was not until 1921 that José Mario Fallótico, in Buenos Aires, Argentina, discovered that the use of a white cane would serve to distinguish him and thus be able to help him; only that José Fallotic never patented his invention, and it was the American George Benham, president of the Illinois Lions Club, who later proposed a white cane with a red lower end for use by the visually impaired, so that they would be given priority step, his proposal was accepted and in a short time the use of this element became universal. Interestingly, Americans celebrate "International White Cane Day" on October 15, but without knowing who its inventor really was. Currently, the cane has become for the blind an extension of their body. Something like an external organ that makes up for his visual deficiencies, providing him with information while he moves the pointer with the ground, since it emits vibrations that are perceived by the user, these vibrations vary their intensity according to the circumstances of the surface, they are they exacerbate when it presents greater hardness, allowing you to interpret the exact situation in which you find yourself. This sensory tool guides the steps of the blind, allowing them to perceive the type of surface they are walking on and at the same time providing information about their context and location within it. There are currently different models of walking sticks on the market, each of which has a different type of grip. Their design is one or the other depending on the service they are intended to provide, depending on the degree of blindness of the user. Thus, the B1 model is designed to guide the totally blind, the B2, those who slightly distinguish between light and shadow, and finally the B3 guides the steps of the blind who still have some residual vision. However, I believe that there is still much to be done, and it would be very useful for the blind person to receive more information, such as a cane implemented with electronic accessories.

Proximity sensor

Ultrasonic sensors have as their main function the detection of objects through the emission and reflection of acoustic waves. They work by emitting an ultrasonic pulse against existing objects, in such a way that upon detecting the reflected pulse, a timer is stopped that started counting when the pulse was emitted. This time is referred to distance and according to the chosen response parameters, it sends a digital or analog electrical signal.

Objectives

General

Detect the distance of an obstacle with the implementation of proximity sensors and give a warning by means of vibrating motors that will increase their intensity with the approach to said obstacle and with a voice indicating the distance from it (as long as the blind person wants it).

Specifics
  • Measure the distance between an obstacle and the blind person to prevent the blind person from having physical contact with it.
  • Convert a frequency signal to voltage in order to increase the intensity with which the vibrating motors work.
  • Save maintenance using a low energy consumption system in order to have a long duration for one day.
Limitations
  • Maximum detection distance of an object is 2 meters.
  • The detection angle is 55 degrees.
  • The maximum voltage of the vibrator motors is 5v.
  • Non-detection of some objects with angles not reachable by the sensors.

Chapter I. Description

1.1 General operation

It is a cane implemented with sensors that will be able to warn the blind that there is an obstacle before making physical contact with it, this will be achieved through small vibrations in the handle of the cane that will increase as you get closer to the obstacle and through a voice that will will indicate the distance of such an object.

1.2 Block diagram

1.2.1 Power supply

In this stage of the circuit, a high-performance 9-volt battery will be implemented to power all the stages of the circuit and the motors.

1.2.2 Load control

In this stage, the power supply to the different parts of the circuit is controlled since they work with different voltages such as sensors, motors, circuits, etc.

1.2.3 Proximity sensor

This stage is the one in charge of sending the signal depending on the distance with which the vibrator will work, since the closer the lens of the proximity sensor is, the greater the frequency with which the signal will bounce and in this way it is intended to convert this signal so that the vibration motor also works at a higher speed and in the case of voice memory to make changes in the phrases.

1.2.4 Microcontroller

The microcontroller is the one in charge of sending pulses and receiving the signal coming from the sensor and from there sending it to the output of P1 to be converted by a DAC (Digital to Analog Converter) and sent to the vibration motor. If this stage is not done it will be very difficult to make it work at the desired speed. It is also responsible for controlling the voice memory of the signals that are going to be sent to it.

1.2.5 DAC

In this stage it is intended to convert the signal coming from the microcontroller so that the motor can work depending on the frequency that is being sent by the sensors, having this a minimum point to start with a small vibration being about 2 meters from the obstacle until a maximum point. with a constant vibration when you are very close to the obstacle.

1.2.6 Power stage

In this stage, what is intended is that the signal obtained from the DAC has a higher power so that the circuit can support the vibrating motors.

1.2.7 Vibration motor

In this stage is where the converted signal arrives to be able to have a speed control that depends on the frequency that the sensor is working, which is then converted to a voltage that will increase as the frequency that it sends increases, once converted into the DAC.

1.2.8 Voice memory

This stage receives a signal from the microcontroller that, depending on the signal sent by the micro, is the phrase that is going to be said.

Chapter II. Analysis and design

2.1 Stages

2.1.1 Power supply

Input power is 9 volts and is provided by a high performance battery that is small and portable which is why that type of battery was chosen.

2.1.2 Load control

This stage is carried out by means of voltage regulators to power the different stages of the circuit such as sensors, integrated circuits and motors, which are the ones that consume the most, reaching the conclusion of a 7805 regulator.

7805 Regulator
2.1.3 Proximity sensor

The sensors to be used are the SRF05, the proximity sensors generate pulses that are put into the micro, which is the one in charge of making the conversion to centimeters. That pulse is a 40KHz burst which is received by the receiving sensor.

Ultrasonic sensor SRF05
Sensor parameters
transmitter

The transmitter is built with a 555 and CMOS inverters, the CMOS feature is used to parallel them and increase the transmit power. The signal coupled between the positive and negative terminals is 180º out of phase, so the voltage applied between the terminals is doubled.

receiver

The receiver is composed of two circuits: signal amplifier and detection circuit. The signal is received by the receiving sensor and amplified 1000 times (60dB) in two steps: one amplifier times 100 (40dB) and one amplifier times 10 (20dB).

Signal detector

The signal detector consists of a half bridge rectifier and a comparator. The comparison voltage is set to 0.045Vdc.

2.1.5 Microcontroller

This stage will control the motors through port 1. When an obstacle approaches, it will send a one or a zero to the outputs, and port 2 will send, as with the motors, a digital signal that, depending on the value, will call the saved message, the memory manufacturer's data sheet must be taken into account to see the maximum recording time.

2.1.5 DAC

This stage was designed by means of the digital output sent by the micro and depending on the output bits, this stage will transform each digital signal into an output voltage. The DAC that was used was the r-2r with resistance values of 470 Ohms for r and 1KOhm for 2r.

2.1.6 Load stage

This stage is made up of a voltage follower and a current amplifier, this was done because what the DAC commands does not raise the motors and with this stage they can be raised. The Operational to be used was the LM358N since it occupies a single source and the transistor to use was the TIP41C since it is a power transistor and can easily withstand vibrating motors.

2.1.7 Vibration motor

This is one of the final stages in which the input voltage that the motors will receive depending on the distance was designed.

2.1.8 Voice memory

In this stage of the voice memory, through the microcontroller, different outputs to the memory were designed in which they depend on the distance of an object, in which the output of the voice memory must be connected to a speaker or , in this case, to a female plug for playback on headphones. This voice memory handles several modalities in which one of them is to work in addressing mode, which consists of recording the voice, indicating binary numbers to the inputs, which was the one used for this project.

4.1.2 Proximity sensor

In this stage, some ultrasonic sensors were carried out, in which at the beginning they had been designed with the transmitter and the receiver but seeing that it gave us the signal with a lot of noise, it was decided to change for a manufactured one (SRF05) reducing the space. In the sensors there were 2 ways to use the first was through two pins, one for the input and the other for the output and the second was for a single pin in which the inputs and outputs are inserted in the same pin. With this connection the sensor uses a single microcontroller line for Echo and Trigger. Care must be taken to configure the microcontroller line as input or output, as appropriate at any given time.

  1. Configure the Echo/Trigger pin as an output.
  2. Apply a pulse of at least 10us to the Echo/Trigger pin.
  3. Configure the Echo/Trigger pin as input.
  4. The module transmits a pulse train of 8 cycles at 40KHz.
  5. Wait for the Echo/Trigger pin to go to level “1”.
  6. Initialize to zero and start the Timer.
  7. When the receiving capsule receives the signal bounced off an object, the Echo/Trigger pin goes back to level “0”.
  8. Measure the duration of the pulse of this signal, that is, the time in which the Echo/Trigger pin remains at "1".
  9. Calculate the distance value based on the recorded time.

5.1.1 Software design

The program for developing the print on the copper plate was made by the Eagle Layout 5.6 program.

  1. The first step is to name the folder where the files will be saved
  2. Once the folder was made, the schematic was made first, once the schematic was finished, it was passed to “board”
  3. Then the components were arranged and in the end the yellow lines were joined by tracks.
5.1.2 Copper Tablet Design

The method used to transfer the circuits to the board was using Couché paper:

  1. First, the copper bakelite board is cleaned
  2. The circuit is printed on the Couché paper
  3. Once printed, the ironing begins. First, align the paper with the plate, do not cut the baking sheet yet, since it is very difficult to keep it from moving when ironing.
  4. Heat the iron to a little more than half and put it on the paper firmly, not moving the paper is very important. 2 or 3 passes is enough. If the iron is very hot or you pass over it many times, the tracks are deformed or the copper is raised.
  5. Check that all the tracks are stuck, one way is to look at the plate against the light in which you will see that where there is a track it is very bright
  6. Then let the copper bakelite board cool with the paper for a few minutes and then put it in a container with water for 10 minutes.
  7. Then with the tip of a finger the paper is removed.
  8. With a rag you can remove what is missing from the paper
  9. Once done, cut the copper bakelite board
  10. Then the circuit is put into the Ferric Chloride in a container with a trickle of water, shaking it until the remaining copper disappears.
